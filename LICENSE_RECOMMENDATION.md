# License Recommendation

Recommended public-license setup:

1. **Code:** MIT License. This is already provided in `LICENSE` and is appropriate for Stata replication code, cleaning scripts, and repository utilities.
2. **Paper and written documentation:** Use one of the following, depending on supervisor/university guidance:
   - `CC BY 4.0` if you want broad reuse with attribution.
   - `CC BY-NC 4.0` if you want reuse with attribution but not commercial reuse.
   - "All rights reserved" until dissertation submission and assessment are complete.
3. **Data:** Do not license third-party raw data yourself. UN DESA, BACI/CEPII, WDI, World Bank, IMF DOTS, and Rauch classification files remain governed by their original terms of use.

Practical recommendation for a research portfolio repository:

- Keep the MIT license for code.
- Do not publicly commit raw data.
- Commit only sanitized regression tables, coefficient summaries, and figures that can be shared under source-data licenses.
- Add a short note in the final paper folder explaining whether the draft is public, embargoed, or available on request.
